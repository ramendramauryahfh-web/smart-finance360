What Is the S&P 500 and How to Invest in It

27/11/2025, 12:10:35 · 10 min read · By R K Maurya · Views

What is the S&P 500?

The S&P 500 (Standard & Poor’s 500) is a stock market index that tracks the share price performance of approximately 500 of the largest publicly traded companies in the United States. It is a capitalisation-weighted index, meaning companies with a larger market value have proportionally more influence on the index’s performance. The S&P 500 is widely regarded as a benchmark for the overall health of the U.S. stock market and is often used as a proxy for the broader U.S. economy.

How Are Companies Selected for the S&P 500?

Not every company can be included in the S&P 500. Companies must meet specific criteria such as being listed on a major U.S. stock exchange, having a sufficiently large market capitalisation, demonstrating adequate liquidity, and having positive recent earnings. A committee from S&P Dow Jones Indices oversees the selection process and may add or remove companies to ensure the index accurately represents the large-cap U.S. market.

What Does the S&P 500 Tell Investors?

The S&P 500 serves multiple purposes for investors:

  • Market Indicator: Movements in the index often reflect overall investor sentiment about the U.S. economy.
  • Diversified Exposure: Investors gain access to multiple sectors, including technology, healthcare, finance, and consumer goods.
  • Benchmark for Performance: Many fund managers use the S&P 500 as a benchmark to compare fund performance.

Historically, the S&P 500 has delivered an average annual return of approximately 9–10% before inflation, though actual returns vary depending on economic cycles.

Why Investors Choose S&P 500 Exposure

Investing in S&P 500 funds is popular due to several advantages:

  • Low Cost and Simplicity: Buying an index fund provides instant exposure to 500 companies without selecting individual stocks.
  • Diversification: Built-in diversification reduces risk compared with holding only a few stocks.
  • Broad Market Representation: The index represents a substantial portion of the total U.S. equity market.
  • Passive Investing Advantage: Index funds are passively managed, resulting in lower fees and less portfolio turnover.

How to Invest in the S&P 500

You cannot directly buy the S&P 500 index because it is a benchmark, not a stock. However, you can gain exposure through:

  • Index Mutual Funds or ETFs: These funds hold the same stocks as the S&P 500 in roughly the same proportions.
  • International Brokerage Platforms: Many brokers allow investors outside the U.S. to purchase U.S.-listed ETFs.
  • Long-Term Buy-and-Hold Strategy: Investing through ETFs or index funds for the long term allows compounding and can lead to wealth creation.

Popular funds tracking the S&P 500 include those offered by major asset management companies, which provide reliable ways to mirror the index’s performance.

Potential Downsides to S&P 500 Investing

  • Average Market Returns: Index funds aim to replicate the S&P 500, so investors receive average market returns rather than outperforming the market.
  • Lack of Customisation: Investing in a broad index means giving up control over individual stock choices.
  • Market Risk: While diversified, the portfolio is still subject to broader market fluctuations and economic downturns.

Is Investing in the S&P 500 Right for You?

Whether the S&P 500 is suitable depends on financial goals and risk tolerance. For investors seeking a simple, diversified, low-maintenance, and long-term investment, S&P 500 funds can serve as a solid foundation. Those desiring more control over specific investments, higher growth potential from select sectors, or personalisation based on values may find a passive index fund too broad.

For beginners, the S&P 500 offers a balanced starting point: diversified, historically reliable, and low cost.

Conclusion

The S&P 500 is a widely followed benchmark that tracks 500 major publicly traded companies in the U.S. By investing through index funds or ETFs, investors gain exposure to a diversified basket of leading companies. For those seeking simplicity, diversification, and long-term growth, S&P 500 funds remain one of the most effective investment tools available. With careful planning and a long-term approach, investing in the S&P 500 can be a cornerstone of a successful portfolio strategy.
